Section 01
DraftKings Position Strategy

DK's full-PPR scoring, yardage bonuses, and 20-man rosters shift optimal draft strategy compared to other platforms. Here's how to adjust at each position.

20 roster spots — 2 extra vs. Underdog
Full PPR — heavily favors pass catchers
+3 pts at 300 passing yards
+3 pts at 100 rush / receiving yards
Flex spot → almost always WR
QBTarget rounds 10–13 + late dart
  • QB is a smaller % of team points in full PPR — other positions score relatively more
  • Rushing QB edge is mitigated by 300-yd bonus boosting pocket passers
  • Exception: Lamar & Daniels still worth projecting 100-yd rush bonus
  • Extra roster spot makes 3-QB builds viable — grab QB3 in rounds 16–20
  • Skip top 10 QBs — take two mid-tier QBs + late-round upside dart
  • Stack partners can override timing — always take value when it appears
RBHero RB or Zero RB
  • Hero: Take an elite RB R1 if available, then wait — only need one more
  • Zero: Load early rounds with WRs/elite TE; use in ~10% of DK portfolio
  • Flex spot should almost never be an RB — full PPR elevates WRs
  • Bijan, Gibbs have league-winning upside — don't pass them up
  • Outside true elites, easier to piecemeal 80% of elite RB production than WR
  • Extra roster spot = more room for a late-round RB flier
WREarly, often, you can't over-invest
  • Expect to start 4 WRs every week — build the deepest room in the draft
  • Opening with 6 straight WRs is a valid strategy in this format
  • Target 9–11 WRs per draft; starters available into round 20
  • Full PPR rewards volume — but don't sacrifice upside for floor alone
  • Complete your QB stacks at WR when possible
  • When in doubt between WR and any other position — take the WR
TENot like QB — full PPR + flex eligible
  • Tier 1: Bowers & McBride — median projections near peak Kelce/Gronk years
  • Loveland — similar upside, far more risk; lacks multi-year track record
  • Mid-tier ~4 TEs: high weekly spike potential but volatile week-to-week
  • Look for TE pockets mid-draft where value exceeds WR ADP at same pick
  • Often grab 2 TEs in mid-rounds, ideally stacked with your QBs
  • Late-round TEs lack youth/athleticism — less excited about 3–4 TE builds this season
Section 02
The Best Ball Decision Pyramid

A weighted guide for on-the-clock decisions. Not a strict decision tree — topics lower in the pyramid carry greater overall weight on advance rates.

Layer 01 — Foundation
Roster Construction
  • Parameters: 2–3 QBs, 4–7 RBs, 6–9 WRs, 2–4 TEs
  • Focus on avoiding negative-advance-rate constructions, not just chasing high-advance ones
  • 3- and 4-RB builds declining in success as WRs move up boards
  • 6–7 RB builds gaining success — teams prioritize WRs early then fill RB with depth
  • Starting heavy at a position early means finishing with fewer total picks at that spot
Layer 02
Stacking
  • Pairing QB with pass catchers increases correlation — limits how much you need to get right
  • Target 1–3 pass catchers and/or 1–2 RBs from same team (usually up to 3 per team)
  • An NFL offense's scoring is correlated all season — stacking is a season-long edge
  • Critical given top-heavy payouts and needing to finish 1/16 in back-to-back playoff weeks
Layer 03
ADP
  • Draft players past their ADP (real-time value) or before their eventual closing ADP
  • ADP becoming more efficient — value of a player falling to you increases each year
  • Close-line value (injured players, free agents) also materially improves advance rates
  • Being right moving a player up two rounds is more valuable than the cost of being wrong two rounds down
Layer 04
Player Takes
  • Hardest to predict but most meaningful — use updated, format-specific rankings
  • Early summer: target players with unclear roles/landing spots (e.g. Aiyuk → Daniels bet)
  • Take chances on undervalued injured players (e.g. Jordyn Tyson if healthy)
  • Lean into binary situations where ADP reflects median outcome (Warren vs. Dowdle)
  • Being wrong is fine — most teams lose. Being right on one player can win a championship
Layer 05
Positional Capital
  • Elite QBs at a discount due to mid-round rushing QBs emerging
  • RBs: hit elite seasons or piecemeal with mid/late-round value
  • WRs: continue drafting early and often
  • Early/mid-round TEs separating from late-rounders with floor + upside combos
  • Use tiers analysis to identify positional strengths and weaknesses across the draft
Layer 06 — Apex
Week 17
  • Game-stack correlation increases Week 17 EV by ~16%
  • Game stacking increases ability to win the finals by ~50%
  • Stack your QB + mix in mini stacks (RB vs. opposing WR)
  • Only ~11% chance of getting one team to finals across 150 max entries — but finals upside is enormous
  • Never sacrifice the layers below it to force a Week 17 stack
